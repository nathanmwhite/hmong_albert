following fable. peb haiv hmoob muaj ib zag dabneeg hu ua, "the young

a young man had been caught in a daring act of theft and had been
condemned to be executed for it.  he expressed his desire to see his
mother, and to speak with her before he was led to execution, and of
course this was granted.  when his mother came to him he said: "i want
to whisper to you, mother" and when she brought her ear near him, he
nearly bit her ear off.  all the bystanders were horrified, and asked
him how he could mean by such brutal and inhuman conduct to his
mother.  "it is to punish her," he said.  "when i was young i began
with stealing little things, and brought them home to mother.  instead
of rebuking and punishing me, she laughed and said: 'it will not be
noticed.' it's because of her that i am the way i am today."
"he is right, people," said a wiseman; "our elders said: "train up a
child in the way he should go; and when he is old he will not depart
there from."
